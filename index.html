THIA

Tech Hiring Innovation Accelorator:Utilizing hiring best practices within the Government

This program guide provides information about the benefits of the program, how agencies can take part, and the experience to enhance agencies ability to hire high-quality technology talent.

Learn more

Program Overview

This program is a four-month immersive training and development initiative to equip federal hiring professionals with the tools, knowledge, and skills necessary to navigate the rapidly-changing landscape of technical recruitment. This program is a collaborative effort led by United States Digital Service (USDS), Performance Policy Management (PPM), Office of Management and Budget, (OMB) , and Office of Personnel Management (OPM), to foster innovation and excellence in federal hiring practices.

Key Program Highlights

In-depth understanding of modern technology, roles, and occupational structures

Strategic alignment of agency hiring needs with available authorities and modern recruitment strategies

Development of talent teams and organizational strategies tailored for technology roles

Mastery of modern recruitment methods, diversity, equity, and inclusion principles, and recruitment marketing strategies

Program Objectives

What you’ll learn:

Throughout this program, you’ll be taught how to translate critical concepts and best practices in the field of technical hiring, into effective, real-world practice. Attracting the best and most qualified technical talent to an organization is not solely a challenge faced by the government. Industry also shares these challenges, as we all recognize the exponential value that superior technical talent can bring to an organization. In order to help overcome these growing needs and inherent challenges, it’s crucial to educate and train hiring stakeholders. It’s also equally important to discuss and educate on commercial trends and technology like AI that are designed to help with speeding up the time to hire, streamlining processes, budget savings, and providing data analysis.

Digital hiring in the 21st century government

Modern recruitment methodologies must include innovative strategies that agencies can use to identify top talent for their openings. Hiring the right candidate for a role has never been more important as Millennials and Generation Z rapidly become the cornerstone of the workforce. With the continual and rapid evolution of technology and work ethics, recruiters now have a wide range of techniques and tools at their disposal to source, recruit, engage, and enhance the candidate search processes. You will learn how to use these tools and best practices to work for you.

Recruiting using modern methods

Developing an organizational talent strategy within the fast paced technology sector can be an extremely challenging task for any organization, but especially so for the federal government that oftentimes faces constraints with factors such as technology, budget, and lack of flexibility allotted with policy, process, procedure, and even sheer size, which can impact time to hire, as well as quality of candidates.

How to hire

Understanding how modern technology roles align with occupational series, General Schedule levels, and pay scales (if not on the General Schedule). Oftentimes Hiring managers don’t know what mechanisms and authorities are at their disposal. Their concerns revolve around budget, time, and identifying the best possible candidate to fit their needs while adhering to their agency's policy and regulations. With well over 100 different hiring authorities, the federal recruitment and hiring process can be both intimidating and a challenge to navigate for many that are involved in the hiring process. You’ll learn how to determine which hiring authorities align to your needs.

Leading change as a hiring professional

You will learn how to craft an effective hiring strategy that your agency can successfully leverage for a wide range of tech skills. You will also meet and network with other agency partners to learn how they have been able to successfully attract, hire, and retain highly sought after tech talent. This will include strategies like candidate pooling and skills based hiring, shared hiring, capturing data and metrics, evaluating success, and building knowledge share/support mechanisms to help you succeed after you’re back within your agency. The goal is to be equipped with essential tools, support, and be able to collaborate with new connections in order to successfully make a meaningful impact to mission critical hiring objectives.

Program Benefits

Best Practices in Federal Hiring: Develop strategies to attract tech talent by utilizing different hiring authorities, increasing agencies' ability to build a diverse workforce through existing mechanisms, and learning methods to reduce the time it takes to hire.

Practical Tools and Techniques: Develop new skills to implement competency frameworks and federal position descriptions that reflect the needs of tech roles at your agency.

Professional Development: Develop your team through hands-on experience that includes training and coaching while they continue in their positions. Participants will have the opportunity to engage with peers from various federal agencies, creating a community of practice and shared learning that extends beyond the program's conclusion.

Enhance Recruitment Skills: Gain in-depth knowledge of digital services, technology infrastructure, and AI roles aligning them with federal job series. Learn to implement an approach that focuses on candidates skill and competencies rather than just qualifications. Learn modern practices to attract candidates that your agency needs most.

Develop a Consultative Framework: Learn and refine how to best collaborate with your hiring partners and develop a consultative approach to hiring. A consultative framework between recruiters and HR ensures aligned goals, improves candidate fit, and fosters collaboration through regular meetings, feedback loops, joint training, and shared metrics. This leads to a more efficient and effective hiring process, attracting, and retaining top talent.

Plan and Design a Talent Team: Learn methods for building and training a talent team that strongly aligns with your agencies’ needs, resulting in the ability to acquire more qualified candidates, and do so more quickly. Subsequently, implement similar processes, technologies, and modern commercial best practices, while training those involved in the hiring process to adopt these methodologies moving forward.

Program Experience

A unique learning experience

Our team of master coaches and educators will lead you through a unique hybrid learning environment designed to immerse you in both the general guidelines and principles as well as the applied practice of hiring for technology and AI roles utilizing modern best practices within the Government.

Expert Educators

Our instructors are experts in the field of Recruitment & Hiring, Human-Centered Design, AI, and Technology and are excited to have you ride alongside, as they share their years of experience and hiring advice with you. Through our blend of remote and onsite learning, you’ll have the opportunity to engage with leaders in modern hiring practices, and learn how to effectively translate methodologies and processes into real world application. You’ll have the opportunity to visit and engage with other agency leaders to learn how they were able to implement these forward thinking hiring practices within the government.

Powerful learning opportunities

Our comprehensive and personalized teaching approach equips you with the necessary tools to succeed, regardless of your learning style. We combine traditional in-person classroom experiences, including seminars, panels, and guest speakers, with flexible, asynchronous online content, such as readings, team projects, and interactive discussions.

Coaching and Mentoring

Our coaches will provide each learner with a personalized multi-pronged training approach combining best practice education techniques by adopting an optimum approach between experiential, coaching and mentoring, and in-classroom learning opportunities to make for an engaging and successful learning experience. We’ll be there with you each step of the way to help provide you with answers, suggestions, and help maximize your learning experience.

Agency Interest

Are you an individual contributor in the hiring process? Are you a CHCO ready to champion for your agency and enhance your team's ability to hire tech talent? Then this opportunity is for you. We invite agencies interested in participating to identify 4-5 participants within their agency to join this cohort training.

Possible roles include:
  1. HR Specialists/Recruiters: These professionals are directly involved in the hiring process and can benefit from training on modern tech hiring practices, skills-based assessments, and effective recruitment strategies.
  2. Hiring Managers: Managers responsible for making hiring decisions should understand the nuances of tech hiring, including aligning agency needs with hiring authorities and crafting tailored hiring strategies.
  3. Diversity and Inclusion Officers: Including these professionals ensures that diversity and inclusion practices are integrated into tech hiring efforts, promoting a more diverse and equitable workforce.
  4. Training and Development Specialists: These individuals can refresh and update their skills-based hiring practices to align training programs with the agencies’ tech workforce needs.
  5. IT Specialists: Including IT specialists or tech experts ensures that learnings from the training program are leveraged with OCIO Officers to support technical aspects of tech hiring, such as utilizing different hiring mechanisms and understanding modern tech skills. This role will also create a collaborative approach between hiring and tech offices that may not currently exist.
  6. Leadership/CHCO: Executive-level participation demonstrates commitment to improving tech hiring practices and ensures alignment with the agencies’ strategic goals.
  7. GC/Accountability (Optional): This person should attend key sessions that touch on the role of GC and how they can be blockers or enhancers to hiring actions. Opportunities will be provided for them to review new tools and processes to set up the teams for success while following agencies’ protocols.

*Note some roles are not required to attend all training but will have insight of the learnings and their roles in a specific hiring action.
